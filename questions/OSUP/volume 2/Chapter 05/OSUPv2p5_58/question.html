Two small, identical conducting spheres repel each other with a force of ${{params.F1}}\rm\ N$ when they are ${{params.d}}\rm\ m$ apart. After a conducting wire is connected between the spheres and then removed, they repel each other with a force of ${{params.F2}}\rm\ N$ (the spheres are still separated by ${{params.d}}\rm\ m$).

What is the original charge on each sphere? Assume that $q_1$ is positive and $q_1>q_2$, where $q_1$ represents the initial charge on sphere 1 and $q_2$ is the initial charge on sphere 2.
